📁 Case Files

Crime A — Rainbow Headline
Unsolved
Every word, a different colour…
⚠ The Crime: Each word a different colour on a shifting gradient — maximum effort, minimum readability.
✅ The FixOne headline colour, maximum contrast. Nike, Apple, Vogue — always single-colour headlines. The words carry the message.
Crime B — Font Catastrophe
Unsolved
How many fonts fit on one poster?
⚠ The Crime: Comic Sans, Courier New, Impact, italic script, bold condensed — plus a toddler, Clippy, and two Renaissance portraits.
✅ The FixMaximum 2 typefaces. Spotify uses one font — "Circular" — for their entire global brand identity.
Crime C — No Visual Hierarchy
Unsolved
Everything is important = nothing is…
⚠ The Crime: All services at identical visual weight. No entry point, no reading order, no flow.
✅ The FixBIG brand → MEDIUM services → small details. Apple posters: one word fills 70% of the space. Everything else is secondary.
Crime D — Alignment Anarchy
Unsolved
Left. Centre. Right. Diagonal. Simultaneously…
⚠ The Crime: No consistent axis. Text switches alignment every line. Plus a spelling error: "COMMING SOON".
✅ The FixOne alignment throughout. The NYT has used the same column grid since 1851. Grid = instant credibility.
🏆

Case Closed

All 4 crimes identified. Good typography is invisible — this poster announced itself from across the room for all the wrong reasons.
